# Slack (Answers)

### 1. How do you join a channel in an organization's Slack workspace? (**Joining & Channels)**

Ans: Slack is a team communication and collaboration platform used by organizations, companies, and teams to communicate and collaborate in the workplace. The steps to join a channel in an organization's Slack workspace are as follows: 

- Firstly, download Slack.
- Create an account on Slack.
- An invitation from your workplace is sent to you; accept it.
- After entering the Workplace, go to the left sidebar and search for the channels, then click “join channel“ to be part of the channel.

The steps above will help you join Slack.

---

### 2. When should you use a **channel** instead of a **direct message**? (**Channel vs Direct Message)**

Ans: In Slack, there are two ways to communicate: channels and direct messages. But in certain scenarios, it is preferred to use a channel instead of a direct message, like 

- When the information is relevant to the group, not just to a single individual.
- If we want to send the same message to multiple people, it is more efficient to send a single message in a channel rather than sending direct messages to multiple users multiple times.
- If the message is important, you want the conversation to be transparent and visible so that others can benefit from it.
- When the discussion is about the projects.

Therefore, the scenarios above show why you should use a channel instead of a direct message.

---

### 3. Why should we use **threads** instead of replying directly in the channel? (**Threaded Replies**)

Ans: A thread is a reply to a specific message in a channel, rather than sending it in the direct chat. Here are the reasons why we should use threads instead of replying directly in the channel.

- It is used to maintain the history.
- To avoid clutter in the channel.
- To keep the related discussions together.
- To enhance productivity.

---

### 4 (a). What is the difference between `@channel`, `@here`, and `@username`? (**Mentions & Notifications**)

Ans: The difference between @channel, @here, and @username is as follows:

| Commands | Who gets notified | When to use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1. @channel | All the members present in the group are notified even if they are inactive or offline. | For Important announcement to the entire team. |
| 2. @here | The members who are active or online only those will be notified. | It is used when we want to take help from the active users. |
| 3. @username | Only the specific person is notified who has been mentioned. | When the message is only for a specific person. |

Therefore, the table above shows the differences between these three commands.

### 4 (b). When should each be used? (**Mentions & Notifications**)

Ans: The usage of @channel, @here, and @username is as follows:

- @channel: This notifies all channel members, even if they are inactive or offline. It is used when the message is important for the entire team.
- @here: Notifies only active, online people in the channel.
- @username: This is used to notify the specific member present in the group.

---

### 5. What is the correct way to share code or logs in Slack? (**Sharing Code & Files**)

Ans: The correct way to share code or logs in Slack is as follows:

**First way:**

- Firstly, open the channel where you want to share code or logs.
- On the left-hand side, there is a ‘+’ sign; click on that.
- There are many options available, such as a list, a text snippet, a workflow, and uploading from your computer.
- Select a text snippet and paste your code there.
- We can also select Upload from your computer to share our code as a file.

**Second way:**

- Open Slack.
- Copy your code.
- Type three backticks (```) in Slack.
- Paste your code.
- Again, type three backticks for the closing purpose.

---

### 6. List **2–3 best practices** you should follow while communicating in an organization’s Slack. (**Professional Slack Etiquette (Important))**

Ans: While communicating in an organization’s Slack, we must follow the following practices:

- We should be very professional and clear while conveying the information.
